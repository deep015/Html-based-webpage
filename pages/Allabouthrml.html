All About HTML

HTML: Hyper Text Markup Language

HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language. It is the standard language used to create and structure the content of a webpage.

  • HyperText: This refers to the ability to create links that connect web pages to one another, making the web a connected "web" of information.
  • Markup Language: This means you use "tags" to surround your content, giving it meaning and structure. You are "marking up" a plain text document.

1. Headings (<h1> to <h6>)

What it is: Headings are tags used to define titles and subtitles on your page. They are crucial for creating a logical hierarchy and outline for your content.

<h1>Heading 1</h1>
<h2>Heading 2</h2>
<h3>Heading 3</h3>
<h4>Heading 4</h4>
<h5>Heading 5</h5>
<h6>Heading 6</h6>
  • <h1>: Most important, typically used once per page.
  • <h2>: Major section heading.
  • <h3>: Sub-section heading under <h2>.

Purpose: Structure your document for humans and machines (like search engines). Not just for making text big.

2. Paragraph (<p>)

Purpose: Group sentences and blocks of text together.

<p>This is the first paragraph.</p>
<p>This is a second, separate paragraph.</p>

3. Horizontal Rules (<hr>)

Defines a thematic break in an HTML page.

<p>This is the first topic.</p>
<hr />
<p>This is a completely different topic.</p>

4. Line Break (<br>)

Creates a line break.

<p>221B Baker Street<br>
London, England</p>

5. Lists (<ul>, <ol>, <li>)

Unordered and ordered lists with nesting:

<ul>
  <li>Tea Bag</li>
  <li>Water</li>
  <li>Sugar</li>
  <li>Milk</li>
</ul>

<ol>
  <li>Boil the water</li>
  <li>Add tea bag, sugar, milk</li>
  <li>Boil 5 minutes</li>
  <li>Serve</li>
</ol>

6. Anchor Tag (<a>)

Used to create hyperlinks:

<a href="https://www.coderarmy.in/" target="_blank">Visit Coder Army</a>
  • _self - opens in same tab (default)
  • _blank - opens in new tab

7. Image Tag (<img>)

Embed images with src and alt attributes:

<img src="https://cdn.britannica.com/16/234216-050-C66F8665/beagle-hound-dog.jpg" 
     height="300px" width="300px" alt="This is Dog Photo">
